President Louw's Speech at WRCA

President Louw will deliver bold action and immediate relief on several critical fronts, underpinning the foundation of our administration's vision. We are committed to fostering a fair and inclusive immigration system, tackling the housing crisis through sustainable development, ensuring universal access to comprehensive healthcare, staunchly defending the freedom of expression, and upholding rigorous standards of fiscal responsibility and transparency in public finance. These initiatives reflect our unwavering dedication to creating a more equitable, prosperous, and resilient society for all.

Speech Transcript

Hello and good morning fellow classmates, and friends. It is with great honor and merriment that I stand here talking before you today. I am delighted to have the opportunity here before you to lay out my team's plans for how we will improve on this great country. Make no mistake this was no feat I could achieve by myself. It took the combination of some of the greatest minds we know to develop such a plan for this country.

Firstly, I would like to expand on what my colleague stated earlier about immigration as well as housing. We are by no means against immigration. We know Canada's historic importance and value as a safe country for those looking for a safe place to immigrate to. However, I would like to point your attention towards Canada’s current housing status. What point is there in immigrating to a country that physically cannot support you or your family. We value the safety and well being of all our immigrants and if we continue expanding our immigration policies at this rate, the housing status will not improve. This not only affects our immigrants but also hinders our own citizens from staying in Canada, as future generations have no chance in this continually growing housing economy. But I can assure you that my team is doing their utmost best to analyze all possible ways to maintain immigration levels while allowing for our housing situation to improve.

Secondly, I would like to point out that our country's current government has poured countless millions of dollars into hundreds of public services over the past few years. Each unrestricted grant is dragging our nation more and more in debt. I along with the Louw Party have spent countless hours and weeks discussing this issue alone. As part of our effort to improve this country we have decided to move in the direction of privatized medical healthcare. This involves the privatization of doctors and hospitals, allowing such medical offices to afford the best technologies rather than having the government fund them while they themselves are slowly struggling to cover necessary costs in each hospital. In addition to this we, as a government, would be able to greatly decrease the medical taxes on our citizens, lifting weight off of your shoulders.

You may have noticed that a large part of our goals to improve Canada have included ways to improve our federal spending deficit. A problem, of which most parties are not making an effort to improve. Ever since our governments over spending under current control during covid we have been struggling to recover the finances spent during this time. This affects our currency and credit around the world, in turn costing us more within global trade and commerce. Part of the reason for this is due to other parties pushing for more and more government control over you and your money. This is why me and my party are dedicated to removing as much governmental restraint over your lives as ethically, morally, and socially possible to allow YOU the freedom to live your life.

The Final topic I will be addressing is the delicate and difficult topic of freedom of expression. Often we are told that we have freedom of speech and the freedom to practice our beliefs and values, UNTIL it goes against what our government likes. As prime minister I would be able to assure you that you will have the freedom to express your beliefs and thoughts without getting cancelled or fully suppressed by the government. Therefore doing away with restrictive laws against freedom of expression… Of course this would not include those related to and about propaganda, indecency, and treason.

If there is one thing I could leave you with it would be this. It is a brilliant quote by Lord Acton, a British historian of the late nineteenth and early twentieth centuries. It goes like this, “All power tends to corrupt, and absolute power corrupts absolutely.” Which is why our goal is to reduce power and temptation to allow for a more clear minded and morally accurate government.
